Gordon: an n-of-1 report tool

**Gordon** is a privacy-first tool for turning personal self-experiments into structured, anonymized n-of-1 reports suitable for scientific analysis.

Gordon is a privacy-first mobile tool that helps individuals turn everyday life and personal self-experiments into structured, anonymized n-of-1 episode reports. Instead of tracking everything all the time, Gordon captures bounded periods (“episodes”), combines passive signals with honest self-reported context, and produces science-compatible summaries that can be shared without exposing raw personal data.

**Core product principles** 

Episodes over diaries
Gordon summarizes periods; it does not log lives.

Approximate beats precise-but-wrong
Weekly recall > fake daily adherence.

Context over identity
Brackets, bins, and durations — never exacts by default.

User attention is scarce
Gordon should be ignorable most of the time.

Artifacts, not platforms
The output is a report, not engagement.

Explicit uncertainty
Every report admits what it cannot know.

Nothing leaves the device without consent
Always visible, always reversible.
